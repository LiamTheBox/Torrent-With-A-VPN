1. Introducing Piracy


You want to pirate because:

  1.  You cannot afford/do not want to pay.
  2.  Is no longer convenient to obtain it legaly.
  3.  The copyright holder does not legaly distribute it anymore.

Is Piracy Morally Correct?

2. Torrenting And Torrent Clients


So what is a Torrent Client?

A torrent Client is software used to dowloand and distribute content by using the Bitorrent protocol, the most used peer-to-peer (P2P) file sharing protocol. This type of dowloading and uploading is very effective, as files are shared with other peoples computers and smartphones and cannot be shutdown by goverments and copyright holders, unless all computers are powered off, kinda like becoming puzzle piece of a decentralised server, as long as there are people who are willing to share the file, there is always someone for replacement.



Is Torrenting Legal?

Yes you can use your Torrent Client to dowloand and share files that are not protected by copyright law or are not illegal or harmful. You really Should not dowloand music and movies without VPN.



Is Torrenting Safe?

Should be, music and videos cannot contain malware and are not executables, but PLEASE BE CAREFUL and always read the file extension before opening the file. The YoutTube Channel The Bread Pirate is a great example of opening hidden malware in a so called "PDF". Similar Story to Linus Tech Tips getting hacked.

Anyway, cracked software can have lots of malware, heres some. Ransomware encrypts your files until you pay with your money or forever, Crypto Miners use your resources to make a profit of your computer and GPU, Becoming part of a Botnet that may be used in DDOS attacks without your knowledge.

**WARNING: Install cracked software at your own risk!**


Torrent Terms

  1.  Peerer: Is downloading the file.
  2.  Seeder: Is sharing the file to the peeres.
  3.  Leecher: Is downloading the file but never shares.
  4.  Swarm: Group of people downloading and sharing a file.
  5.  Tracker: Server that tracks connected users in a torrent.

3. Virtual Private Networks (VPNs)


And what is a VPN?

short for "Virtual Private Network", it's a service and software that hides your IP address and makes you virtually untraceable from your Internet Service Provider (ISP), Goverment and Copyright Trolls. This is because all actions and connections are made in the VPN and are encrypted, So nobody expect your VPN provider can see what you are doing.

Most VPN providers do not keep logs for the purpose of the service, even if 3rd parties requested customer data, they most likely won't be able to find anything that leads to your IP address of origin.



Are VPNs Legal and Safe?

Yes they are! VPNs have always been used by corporations and businesses for data protection, secure remote access and secure communication.



What if I don't use a VPN?

What do you think will happen when the copyright troll gets your IP address while torrenting (Seeding & Peering)? That's right! they get in contact with your ISP and send a nice DMCA letter telling you to stop torrenting, sometimes it can go as far as getting theatened to pay a "fine" that doesn't exist and a lawsuit against you after paying for the fine or admiting guilt (You should probably never do this)

5. Link Torrent Client With VPN


  1.  Open qBitorrent
  2.  Go to “tools” on the top left
  3.  Click on “Preferences”
  4.  Click on “Advanced”
  5.  Select “Network Interface”
  6.  Select the name of your VPN (wg-mullvad if you use Mullvad)
  7.  You are done!

6. Ensure Torrent Client Is Linked With VPN


Get Your Primary Device Ready (Computer)

  1.  In your primary device, turn on your VPN
  2.  Visit ipleak.net
  3.  Scroll down and find "Torrent Address Detection"
  4.  Click “Activate”
  5.  Click “this Magnet Link”
  6.  Choose your Torrent Client
  7.  Click “Open Link”
  8.  Torrent client opens a setup download window. Just click “OK” for now, as you are not dowloading a file that should go inside a “Torrents” or “qBitorrent” folder
  9.  Go back to your browser and wait 10 seconds
  10.  Check your "Torrent Address" is the same as"Your IP Addresses", at least one of those should be the same (IPv4 and/or IPv6)

Get Your Secondary Device Ready (Smartphone)

  1.  Turn on your smartphone or another computer
  2.  Connect your secondary device to your Network by Wi-Fi or Ethernet
  3.  Disable any VPN connections you have on your secondary device
  4.  Visit DuckDuckGo
  5.  type “ip address” in the search bar
  6.  Confirm your device's IP address is different from your computer's IP address
  7.  You are finished! If not, go to step 8
  8.  If this is false or both devices have the same IP address, check your main device's VPN app status.
